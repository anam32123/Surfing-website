Notable surfers:

Hawai’i, California, Florida, Australia, South Africa, Brazil, and France are the countries that produce some of the best surfers. See below some of the most notable surfers of all time.

Andy Irons - 3x World Champion and amazing Hawaiian surfer. He helped redefine and push forward the sport during his lifetime.

John John Florence - 2x World Champion and one of the best surfers in the world. Hailing from the North Shore of Hawai'i, he is extremely versatile and has won two of this year's CT events so far.

Carissa Moore - currently the World #1, this Hawaiian surfer has claimed the World Title 3 times and just took out her first win of the season at Jeffrey’s Bay. She is known for her aggressive and powerful surfing, as well as her work in mentoring young female surfers through her program Moore Aloha

Stephanie Gilmore - 7-time World Champion, tied for the record with fellow Australian Layne Beachley. She is notable for her stylish surfing.

Kai Lenny - this Hawaiian is an all-around waterman, specializing in big-wave surfing, stand-up paddleboarding, windsurfing, and hydrofoiling. This year, he placed third on the WSL Big Wave Tour. He is one of the individuals pushing forward the sport of big-wave surfing, experimenting with aerials and other moves typically not performed on large waves.

Keala Kennelly - Big Wave World Champion Keala Kennelly competed on the WSl CT for ten years for turning her focus onto acting and big-wave surfing. This year, she became the first openly LGBTQ+ World Champion. She has spearheaded the movement for gender equality in the surfing world, fighting for female inclusion in big wave events. It was thanks to her work along with other female big wave surfers that the WSL announced pay parity for men and women athletes. No matter what, Keala always charges and gives her all to her surfing, no matter the risk.
